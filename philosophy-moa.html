Philosophy & Memorandum of Association

The foundational philosophy, objectives, and governing principles of Anandmayee Institute for Entrepreneurship Development & Research (AIEDR).

Institutional Philosophy

Anandmayee Institute for Entrepreneurship Development & Research (AIEDR) is founded on the belief that entrepreneurship, education, and research are central to sustainable economic development, institutional resilience, and social advancement. The Institute seeks to cultivate intellectual capital, ethical leadership, and applied knowledge through structured academic and professional programmes.

AIEDR operates as a not-for-profit academic institution under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013. The Institute is committed to reinvesting all surplus resources towards academic development, research capacity building, faculty enrichment, and institutional infrastructure.

The Institute’s philosophy integrates academic rigor with real-world relevance, emphasizing multidisciplinary learning across entrepreneurship, finance, management, accounting, capital markets, and applied technical domains. AIEDR promotes evidence-based teaching, ethical governance, and long-term institutional stewardship.

Memorandum of Association (MoA)

The Memorandum of Association defines the scope, purpose, and objectives for which Anandmayee Institute for Entrepreneurship Development & Research has been established.

A. Main Objects

  • To establish, promote, and operate educational, academic, research, and training institutions for the advancement of entrepreneurship, management, finance, accounting, capital markets, and allied disciplines.
  • To design and deliver academic programmes, executive education, professional certifications, workshops, seminars, and capacity-building initiatives for students, professionals, entrepreneurs, and institutions.
  • To promote research, innovation, case development, policy analysis, and publication in areas related to entrepreneurship development, financial markets, business management, and institutional governance.
  • To collaborate with universities, academic institutions, government bodies, regulatory authorities, industry associations, and international organizations for academic exchange, joint research, and knowledge dissemination.
  • To establish research centers, incubation hubs, and knowledge platforms that support entrepreneurship, start-ups, and institutional development.

B. Ancillary & Incidental Objects

  • To publish books, journals, working papers, research reports, policy briefs, and educational materials in print or digital form.
  • To organize conferences, academic conclaves, faculty development programmes, and continuing education initiatives.
  • To establish libraries, digital repositories, and learning management systems for academic use.
  • To grant scholarships, fellowships, research grants, and academic awards in furtherance of the Institute’s objectives.
  • To raise funds through lawful means including grants, donations, subscriptions, endowments, and sponsorships consistent with Section 8 regulations.

C. Governance & Non-Profit Character

AIEDR is established as a company limited by guarantee under Section 8 of the Companies Act, 2013. The income and property of the Institute shall be applied solely towards the promotion of its objects and no portion shall be distributed as dividends to its members.

The governance of the Institute shall be carried out by its Board of Directors and Academic Leadership in accordance with applicable laws, internal statutes, and principles of transparency, accountability, and ethical administration.

D. Core Values

  • Academic integrity and intellectual honesty
  • Ethical leadership and institutional responsibility
  • Research-driven decision making
  • Inclusivity, access, and capacity building
  • Long-term societal and economic impact

E. Commitment to Public Purpose

AIEDR is committed to serving the public interest by contributing to human capital development, entrepreneurship promotion, and knowledge creation. The Institute aligns its activities with national priorities in education, skill development, and innovation.
